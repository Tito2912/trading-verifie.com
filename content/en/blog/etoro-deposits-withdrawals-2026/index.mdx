Many users read the signup page and trading fees. Few study **how money goes in and out**. Yet deposits and withdrawals are where a lot of practical friction appears.

## What to verify before the first deposit

Check these 4 points first:

1. Which currency does the account effectively use?
2. Which payment method are you using?
3. Can FX conversion add costs?
4. Will that same method make withdrawals easier later?

An easy deposit does not guarantee an easy withdrawal.

## The real friction points

### Account currency

If your funding source and account currency do not align, you may face:

- FX costs,
- less favorable conversion,
- confusing performance tracking.

### Verification and compliance

Withdrawals may trigger extra checks:

- identity confirmation,
- supporting documents,
- payment method consistency,
- anti-fraud review.

This is normal. It just needs to be expected.

### Visible and hidden costs

Users focus on fixed fees. But indirect costs also matter:

- FX conversion,
- spread when closing positions,
- timing costs when you need cash quickly.

## Good practice: think about the withdrawal first

Before the first deposit, know:

- which method you would use to withdraw,
- which documents might be required,
- what the total expected cost could be,
- what a realistic time frame looks like.

## Bottom line

eToro is not unique here: deposits and withdrawals deserve more attention than most beginners give them. If you understand currency, fees, delays and verification logic, you already remove many avoidable mistakes.
